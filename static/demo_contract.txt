VENDOR SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Vendor Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2025, by and between
Nexora Technologies Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 500 Innovation
Drive, San Francisco, California 94105 ("Company"), and DataBridge Solutions LLC, a Texas limited
liability company with its principal place of business at 1200 Commerce Blvd, Austin, Texas 78701
("Vendor").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company desires to engage Vendor to provide certain data processing and analytics services;
and WHEREAS, Vendor desires to provide such services to Company on the terms and conditions set forth
herein; NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree
as follows:


ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Services" means the data ingestion, transformation, analytics pipeline, and reporting services
described in Exhibit A attached hereto.

1.2 "Deliverables" means all work product, reports, software, code, documentation, and other materials
created by Vendor specifically for Company under this Agreement.

1.3 "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one party to the other
in connection with this Agreement, whether disclosed orally, in writing, or by any other means, that
is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the
nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

1.4 "Intellectual Property Rights" means all patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and
any other proprietary rights recognized in any jurisdiction worldwide.

1.5 "Effective Date" means January 15, 2025.


ARTICLE 2 — SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES

2.1 Scope of Services. Vendor shall perform the Services as described in Exhibit A in accordance with
the timelines, specifications, and quality standards set forth therein. Vendor shall assign qualified
personnel with appropriate expertise to perform the Services.

2.2 Change Orders. Any modification to the scope of Services must be agreed upon in writing by both
parties through a signed Change Order. Vendor shall not perform any additional services beyond the
agreed scope without prior written authorization from Company.

2.3 Subcontracting. Vendor shall not subcontract any portion of the Services to a third party without
the prior written consent of Company. Any approved subcontractor shall be bound by obligations no less
restrictive than those imposed on Vendor under this Agreement.


ARTICLE 3 — COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 Fees. Company shall pay Vendor the fees set forth in Exhibit B. All fees are stated in United
States dollars.

3.2 Payment Terms. Company shall pay all undisputed invoices within thirty (30) days of receipt of
a properly submitted invoice. Invoices shall be submitted monthly in arrears.

3.3 Late Payments. Undisputed amounts not paid within thirty (30) days of the invoice date shall
accrue interest at the lesser of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or the maximum rate
permitted by applicable law.

3.4 Disputed Invoices. Company shall notify Vendor in writing of any disputed invoice amounts within
fifteen (15) days of receipt. The parties shall work in good faith to resolve any such dispute within
thirty (30) days.

3.5 Taxes. Each party shall be responsible for taxes imposed on its own income. Vendor shall be
solely responsible for all employment taxes and withholding related to its personnel.


ARTICLE 4 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1 Assignment of Deliverables. Vendor hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and
interest in and to all Deliverables, including all Intellectual Property Rights therein. Vendor agrees
to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably required by Company to perfect, record, or
enforce such assignment in any jurisdiction. All Deliverables shall be considered works made for hire
to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law; to the extent they do not qualify as works made
for hire, Vendor assigns all rights therein to Company.

4.2 Vendor Background IP. Vendor retains ownership of any pre-existing tools, methodologies,
frameworks, and know-how developed independently of this Agreement ("Background IP"). To the extent
any Deliverable incorporates Background IP, Vendor grants Company a perpetual, irrevocable,
royalty-free, worldwide license to use such Background IP solely as incorporated in the Deliverables.

4.3 License Grant to Vendor Tools. If Vendor uses any third-party software or open-source components
in the Deliverables, Vendor shall identify all such components and ensure that Company's use of the
Deliverables does not violate any third-party license terms.


ARTICLE 5 — CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 Obligations. Each party agrees to: (a) hold the other party's Confidential Information in strict
confidence using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in
no event less than reasonable care; (b) not disclose the other party's Confidential Information to
any third party without prior written consent; and (c) use the other party's Confidential Information
solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement.

5.2 Exceptions. The obligations in Section 5.1 do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes
publicly available through no fault of the receiving party; (b) was rightfully known to the receiving
party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed by the receiving party without reference to
the Confidential Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law or court order, provided that
the receiving party gives the disclosing party prompt written notice and reasonably cooperates in
seeking a protective order.

5.3 Term of Confidentiality. The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Article 5 shall
survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.


ARTICLE 6 — REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

6.1 Vendor Representations. Vendor represents and warrants that: (a) it has full power and authority
to enter into this Agreement; (b) the Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike
manner consistent with industry standards; (c) the Deliverables will conform to the specifications
set forth in Exhibit A for a period of ninety (90) days following acceptance by Company ("Warranty
Period"); and (d) the Deliverables will not infringe any third-party Intellectual Property Rights.

6.2 Disclaimer. EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH IN SECTION 6.1, VENDOR MAKES NO WARRANTIES,
EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR
A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, OR NON-INFRINGEMENT. VENDOR DOES NOT WARRANT THAT THE SERVICES OR
DELIVERABLES WILL BE ERROR-FREE OR UNINTERRUPTED.


ARTICLE 7 — INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Vendor Indemnification. Vendor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Company and its
officers, directors, employees, agents, successors, and assigns from and against any and all claims,
damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out
of or relating to: (a) any breach of Vendor's representations, warranties, or obligations under this
Agreement; (b) any negligent or wrongful act or omission of Vendor or its personnel; (c) any claim
that the Deliverables infringe any third-party Intellectual Property Rights; or (d) any violation of
applicable law by Vendor. Vendor's indemnification obligations shall apply regardless of whether the
claim arises in contract, tort, strict liability, or otherwise, and shall not be limited by any
applicable insurance coverage.

7.2 Company Indemnification. Company shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Vendor from and
against claims arising out of Company's gross negligence or willful misconduct in connection with
this Agreement.

7.3 Indemnification Procedure. The indemnified party shall: (a) promptly notify the indemnifying
party in writing of any claim; (b) give the indemnifying party sole control of the defense and
settlement of such claim; and (c) provide reasonable cooperation to the indemnifying party at the
indemnifying party's expense.


ARTICLE 8 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

8.1 Exclusion of Consequential Damages. IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR
ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS
OF PROFITS, LOSS OF REVENUE, LOSS OF DATA, LOSS OF GOODWILL, OR COST OF SUBSTITUTE GOODS OR
SERVICES, EVEN IF SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

8.2 Cap on Liability. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS
AGREEMENT, WHETHER IN CONTRACT, TORT (INCLUDING NEGLIGENCE), STRICT LIABILITY, OR OTHERWISE, SHALL
NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY COMPANY TO VENDOR DURING THE THREE (3) MONTH PERIOD
IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO SUCH LIABILITY. THIS LIMITATION SHALL APPLY
NOTWITHSTANDING ANY FAILURE OF ESSENTIAL PURPOSE OF ANY LIMITED REMEDY.

8.3 Exceptions. The limitations in Sections 8.1 and 8.2 shall not apply to: (a) a party's
indemnification obligations; (b) damages arising from gross negligence or willful misconduct; or
(c) a party's breach of its confidentiality obligations.


ARTICLE 9 — TERM AND TERMINATION

9.1 Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of
two (2) years ("Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article 9. Upon
expiration of the Initial Term and each subsequent renewal term, this Agreement shall automatically
renew for successive one (1) year periods unless either party provides the other with written notice
of non-renewal no later than ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

9.2 Termination for Convenience. Company may terminate this Agreement or any Statement of Work at
any time for any reason or no reason, with or without cause, upon fifteen (15) days' prior written
notice to Vendor. In the event of termination for convenience by Company, Company shall pay Vendor
only for Services actually performed and accepted up to the effective date of termination; no further
amounts shall be owed.

9.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice
if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within
thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in detail; (b) becomes
insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver appointed; or
(c) ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business.

9.4 Effect of Termination. Upon termination or expiration: (a) Vendor shall promptly return or
destroy all Company Confidential Information; (b) each party shall pay all amounts due and owing as
of the termination date; and (c) all provisions that by their nature should survive shall survive,
including Articles 4, 5, 7, 8, 10, and 11.


ARTICLE 10 — NON-SOLICITATION AND NON-COMPETITION

10.1 Non-Solicitation of Personnel. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twenty-four
(24) months following its expiration or termination, neither party shall, directly or indirectly,
solicit, recruit, or hire any employee or contractor of the other party who was involved in the
performance of this Agreement, without the prior written consent of the other party.

10.2 Non-Competition. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of eighteen (18) months
following its expiration or termination, Vendor shall not, directly or indirectly, provide services
that are substantially similar to the Services to any direct competitor of Company operating in the
same geographic markets. For purposes of this Section, "direct competitor" means any entity that
derives more than twenty percent (20%) of its revenues from products or services that compete
directly with Company's core product offerings as of the date of this Agreement.

10.3 Remedies. The parties acknowledge that any breach of this Article 10 would cause irreparable
harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy, and that the non-breaching party
shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction, without the requirement of posting
any bond.


ARTICLE 11 — MOST FAVORED CUSTOMER

11.1 Most Favored Customer Pricing. Vendor represents and warrants that the fees charged to Company
under this Agreement are no less favorable than the lowest fees charged by Vendor to any other
customer for substantially similar services in substantially similar volumes during the term of this
Agreement. If Vendor offers or provides substantially similar services to any other customer at lower
fees or on more favorable terms, Vendor shall immediately notify Company and automatically reduce
Company's fees to match such lower fees or more favorable terms, retroactive to the date such
lower fees were first offered to such other customer.

11.2 Annual Pricing Review. In connection with each renewal of this Agreement, Vendor shall provide
Company with a written certification confirming that Company continues to receive the most favored
customer pricing as described in Section 11.1, along with supporting documentation as reasonably
requested by Company.


ARTICLE 12 — AUDIT RIGHTS

12.1 Audit Rights. Company and its authorized representatives shall have the right, upon fifteen (15)
days' prior written notice, to audit Vendor's books, records, and systems to verify Vendor's
compliance with the terms of this Agreement, including pricing, data handling practices, and
security controls. Vendor shall cooperate fully with any such audit and provide reasonable access to
its facilities and records. Audits shall be conducted no more than twice per calendar year during
normal business hours.

12.2 Security Assessments. Company reserves the right to conduct, or commission a third party to
conduct, an annual security assessment of Vendor's information security controls, including
penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, and review of security policies and procedures. Vendor
shall remediate any critical or high-severity findings within thirty (30) days and material findings
within ninety (90) days of notification.


ARTICLE 13 — INSURANCE

13.1 Insurance Requirements. During the term of this Agreement, Vendor shall maintain, at its own
expense, the following insurance coverages with insurers reasonably acceptable to Company: (a)
Commercial General Liability Insurance with limits of not less than $2,000,000 per occurrence and
$4,000,000 in the aggregate; (b) Professional Liability (Errors & Omissions) Insurance with limits
of not less than $2,000,000 per claim; (c) Cyber Liability Insurance with limits of not less than
$2,000,000 per occurrence; and (d) Workers' Compensation Insurance as required by applicable law.

13.2 Evidence of Insurance. Upon request, Vendor shall provide Company with certificates of
insurance and additional insured endorsements. Vendor shall provide thirty (30) days' prior written
notice of cancellation or material reduction in coverage.


ARTICLE 14 — DATA PROTECTION AND SECURITY

14.1 Data Security. Vendor shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable technical and
organizational security measures to protect Company data from unauthorized access, disclosure, or
destruction, including encryption of data in transit and at rest, access controls, and regular
security assessments.

14.2 Data Breach Notification. In the event of any unauthorized access to or disclosure of Company
data ("Security Incident"), Vendor shall notify Company within forty-eight (48) hours of discovery,
provide a detailed description of the incident, cooperate with Company's investigation, and take
immediate steps to contain and remediate the incident.

14.3 Data Retention and Deletion. Upon termination of this Agreement or upon Company's request,
Vendor shall promptly and securely delete or return all Company data in its possession and certify
such deletion in writing within thirty (30) days.


ARTICLE 15 — FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 Force Majeure Event. Neither party shall be liable for any delay or failure to perform its
obligations under this Agreement to the extent that such delay or failure is caused by circumstances
beyond such party's reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics,
government actions, war, terrorism, labor disputes, or failures of third-party infrastructure
providers ("Force Majeure Event").

15.2 Notice and Mitigation. A party seeking to invoke force majeure shall: (a) promptly notify the
other party in writing upon becoming aware of the Force Majeure Event, specifying the nature and
expected duration; (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of such event and
resume performance as soon as practicable; and (c) provide regular updates regarding the status of
the Force Majeure Event.

15.3 Extended Force Majeure. If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive
days, either party may terminate this Agreement upon fifteen (15) days' written notice without
further liability, other than payment for Services already performed and accepted.


ARTICLE 16 — GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

16.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws
of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

16.2 Dispute Resolution. In the event of any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement,
the parties shall first attempt to resolve the dispute through good-faith negotiation between senior
representatives of each party. If the dispute is not resolved within thirty (30) days of written
notice of the dispute, either party may pursue its remedies in the courts of competent jurisdiction
as set forth in Section 16.3.

16.3 Jurisdiction and Venue. Each party hereby irrevocably consents to the exclusive jurisdiction
and venue of the state and federal courts located in New Castle County, Delaware for any legal
action arising under this Agreement and waives any objection to such venue.

16.4 Waiver of Jury Trial. EACH PARTY HEREBY IRREVOCABLY WAIVES ITS RIGHT TO A TRIAL BY JURY IN
ANY ACTION OR PROCEEDING ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT.


ARTICLE 17 — GENERAL PROVISIONS

17.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with all Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement
between the parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous
agreements, representations, and understandings.

17.2 Amendments. This Agreement may not be amended except by a written instrument signed by
authorized representatives of both parties.

17.3 Waiver. No failure or delay by either party in exercising any right under this Agreement shall
constitute a waiver of such right.

17.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable,
the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

17.5 Notices. All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by: (a) hand; (b)
overnight courier; or (c) certified mail, return receipt requested, to the addresses set forth in
the preamble. Notices shall be deemed given upon receipt.

17.6 Anti-Assignment. Neither party may assign this Agreement or any of its rights or obligations
hereunder, in whole or in part, whether by operation of law (including by merger, acquisition, or
change of control) or otherwise, without the prior written consent of the other party, which consent
shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed. Any purported assignment in violation of this Section
shall be null and void. Notwithstanding the foregoing, Company may assign this Agreement without
Vendor's consent to an affiliate or in connection with a merger or acquisition of all or substantially
all of Company's assets, provided that Company provides Vendor written notice of such assignment
within ten (10) days.

17.7 Change of Control. Vendor shall notify Company in writing within five (5) business days of any
change of control of Vendor, including any merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all
of Vendor's assets or equity. Company shall have the right to terminate this Agreement without
penalty within thirty (30) days of receiving notice of such change of control.

17.8 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in one or more counterparts, each of which shall
be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid.


EXHIBIT A — SCOPE OF SERVICES

Vendor shall provide the following services:
1. Data ingestion pipeline supporting structured and semi-structured data formats (CSV, JSON, Parquet)
2. Automated data quality validation with anomaly detection and alerting
3. Analytics dashboard with real-time reporting and export functionality
4. Monthly performance reports summarizing key metrics and trend analysis
5. API integration services connecting Company's internal systems to Vendor's platform
6. Dedicated technical support (8x5 during business hours) with 4-hour response SLA for critical issues


EXHIBIT B — FEES

Monthly Platform Fee: $18,500
Implementation Fee (one-time): $45,000
Additional API calls beyond 10M/month: $0.002 per call
Support tier upgrade (24x7): $4,000/month additional

All fees subject to annual increase not to exceed 4% per year with 60 days' prior written notice.
