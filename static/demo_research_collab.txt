JOINT RESEARCH COLLABORATION AND REVENUE SHARING AGREEMENT

This Joint Research Collaboration and Revenue Sharing Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as
of June 10, 2025, by and between Helix BioAnalytics Corporation, a Massachusetts corporation with
its principal place of business at 150 Kendall Square, Cambridge, Massachusetts 02142 ("Helix"),
and Stratum Diagnostics GmbH, a German limited liability company with its principal place of
business at Musterstraße 12, 80331 Munich, Germany ("Stratum"). Helix and Stratum are referred
to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."


ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Research Program" means the joint research and development activities described in Exhibit A
aimed at developing next-generation genomic sequencing assays for oncology diagnostics.

1.2 "Joint IP" means any invention, discovery, know-how, data, or other intellectual property
conceived, created, or reduced to practice jointly by employees or contractors of both Parties
in the course of the Research Program.

1.3 "Background IP" means all intellectual property owned or controlled by a Party prior to the
Effective Date, or developed by that Party independently of the Research Program.

1.4 "Net Revenue" means gross revenues received from commercialization of any product or service
embodying Joint IP, less returns, allowances, taxes, import duties, freight, and insurance costs
directly attributable to such revenues.

1.5 "Commercialization Territory" means the United States, the European Union, the United Kingdom,
Canada, Australia, Japan, and South Korea.


ARTICLE 2 — JOINT RESEARCH PROGRAM

2.1 Scope. The Parties shall conduct the Research Program as described in Exhibit A, contributing
personnel, equipment, and resources as specified therein. Each Party shall designate a project
lead responsible for coordinating its contributions to the Research Program.

2.2 Steering Committee. The Parties shall establish a Joint Steering Committee ("JSC") consisting
of three (3) representatives from each Party. The JSC shall meet quarterly to review progress,
allocate resources, and approve any material changes to the Research Program.

2.3 Records and Reporting. Each Party shall maintain complete and accurate records of all research
activities, data, and results generated under the Research Program. The Parties shall share all
such records with the other Party on a monthly basis.


ARTICLE 3 — JOINT INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY OWNERSHIP

3.1 Joint Ownership. All Joint IP developed under this Agreement shall be jointly owned by the
Parties in equal, undivided shares, with each Party holding a fifty percent (50%) ownership interest.
The Parties shall cooperate in filing, prosecuting, and maintaining patent applications covering
Joint IP in the Commercialization Territory. All costs of such filings shall be shared equally.

3.2 Exploitation Rights. Each Party shall have the right to exploit the jointly owned intellectual
property without accounting to the other Party, subject to the revenue sharing obligations set
forth in Article 4. Neither Party may license Joint IP to a third party without the prior written
consent of the other Party.

3.3 Co-Ownership Administration. The Parties shall enter into a separate joint ownership agreement
within ninety (90) days of the Effective Date to govern the administration of Joint IP, including
prosecution strategy, maintenance decisions, and enforcement against third-party infringers.
Decisions regarding enforcement of jointly owned patent rights shall require mutual written
agreement. Each joint inventor shall execute assignments confirming joint inventorship as required.

3.4 Background IP. Each Party retains sole ownership of its respective Background IP. No license
to Background IP is granted under this Agreement except as expressly set forth herein.

3.5 License to Background IP. Each Party hereby grants to the other Party a non-exclusive,
royalty-free license to use its Background IP solely to the extent necessary to conduct the
Research Program during the term of this Agreement. Such license shall not extend to any
commercialization activities outside the Research Program.


ARTICLE 4 — REVENUE AND PROFIT SHARING

4.1 Revenue Share. The Parties shall share all Net Revenue derived from the commercialization
of products and services based on Joint IP in the following proportions: sixty percent (60%) to
Helix and forty percent (40%) to Stratum ("Revenue Share"). The Revenue Share allocation reflects
each Party's respective contribution of personnel and resources to the Research Program as set
forth in Exhibit A.

4.2 Reporting and Payment. Each Party that receives Net Revenue shall provide the other Party
with a detailed written revenue report within thirty (30) days following the end of each calendar
quarter, accompanied by payment of the other Party's applicable revenue share. Revenue reports
shall include a breakdown of gross revenues, permitted deductions, Net Revenue calculation, and
the applicable Revenue Share payment.

4.3 Profit Sharing for Joint Products. In addition to the Revenue Share in Section 4.1, the
Parties shall share net profits from any jointly commercialized product ("Joint Product") in equal
fifty percent (50%) shares. Net profits shall mean Net Revenue from Joint Products less all direct
and attributable indirect costs incurred by either Party in connection with the development,
manufacture, marketing, and sale of such Joint Products, as agreed upon in the applicable
commercialization plan.

4.4 Royalties from Third-Party Licenses. If the Parties mutually agree to license any Joint IP
to a third party, royalties received from such third-party licenses shall be shared between the
Parties in the same Revenue Share proportions set forth in Section 4.1.

4.5 Audit Rights for Revenue Verification. Each Party shall have the right, exercisable once per
calendar year upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice, to audit the other Party's books, records,
and accounts to verify the accuracy of revenue reports and Revenue Share payments. Audits shall be
conducted by an independent certified public accountant reasonably acceptable to both parties.


ARTICLE 5 — EXCLUSIVITY AND NON-COMPETE

5.1 Exclusivity. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of eighteen (18) months
following its expiration or termination ("Exclusivity Period"), each Party agrees, on an exclusive
basis, not to conduct research, develop, or commercialize any product or technology that is
substantially similar to the Joint IP outside of the Research Program, without the prior written
consent of the other Party. The Parties acknowledge that this exclusivity is a material inducement
for each Party entering into this Agreement and reflects the significant investment each is making.

5.2 Non-Competition. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twenty-four (24) months
following its expiration or termination, neither Party shall, directly or indirectly, engage in
any business activity that competes directly with the commercial applications of the Joint IP in
the oncology diagnostics field within the Commercialization Territory. For the purposes of this
Section, "compete directly" means offering a product or service that performs substantially the
same diagnostic function as a Joint Product and targets the same clinical indications.

5.3 No-Solicit of Employees. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twenty-four
(24) months following its expiration or termination, neither Party shall, directly or indirectly,
solicit, recruit, or hire any employee of the other Party who was involved in the Research Program,
without the prior written consent of the other Party. This provision does not prohibit general
employment advertising not specifically targeted at the other Party's personnel.

5.4 Non-Solicitation of Customers. Neither Party shall, during the term or for a period of twelve
(12) months following termination, directly solicit or approach any customer or commercial partner
of the other Party that was introduced in connection with the Research Program, for the purpose of
entering into a competing arrangement.

5.5 Non-Disparagement. Each Party agrees, during the term and for a period of three (3) years
following termination, not to make, publish, or authorize any negative, disparaging, or
defamatory statements, whether oral or written, about the other Party, its products, services,
leadership, or employees, in any public forum, including press releases, social media, scientific
publications, or investor communications. This provision does not restrict either Party from making
truthful statements in connection with a legal proceeding, regulatory inquiry, or compliance
disclosure required by applicable law.


ARTICLE 6 — MOST FAVORED NATION AND ROFR

6.1 Most Favored Nation Licensing Terms. If either Party grants a third party a license to any
Joint IP on terms that are materially more favorable than those offered to the other Party under
this Agreement, the Party granting such license shall promptly notify the other Party and
automatically extend to the other Party the benefit of those more favorable terms. Each Party
warrants that no license to Joint IP has been granted or is currently pending on terms more
favorable than those set forth in this Agreement.

6.2 Right of First Negotiation. If either Party (the "Offering Party") proposes to sell, assign,
or exclusively license all or substantially all of its interest in any Joint IP to a third party,
the Offering Party shall first provide written notice to the other Party (the "ROFN Notice"),
describing the proposed disposition in reasonable detail. The other Party shall have forty-five
(45) days following receipt of the ROFN Notice to commence good-faith negotiations with the
Offering Party regarding such acquisition or license. If the Parties do not execute a definitive
agreement within ninety (90) days following the ROFN Notice, the Offering Party may proceed with
the proposed transaction with a third party on terms no more favorable to the third party than
those last offered to the other Party.

6.3 Right of First Refusal for Future Research. If either Party identifies an opportunity to
conduct additional joint research related to the subject matter of the Research Program and wishes
to engage a third party for such research, such Party shall first offer the other Party the right
of first refusal to participate in such additional research on the same terms and conditions
proposed for the third party. The other Party shall have thirty (30) days to exercise this ROFR.


ARTICLE 7 — COVENANT NOT TO SUE

7.1 Covenant. Each Party hereby grants to the other Party a covenant not to sue the other Party
or its Affiliates for infringement of any of its solely owned Background IP that is necessarily
practiced by the other Party in conducting the Research Program as described in Exhibit A. This
covenant not to sue is limited to uses within the scope of the Research Program and does not
extend to any commercial use of Background IP outside the Research Program.

7.2 Scope. For the avoidance of doubt, this covenant not to sue shall not prevent either Party
from bringing claims for breach of contract, misappropriation of trade secrets, or infringement
of Background IP that occurs outside the scope of the Research Program. Any purported waiver of
rights beyond this limited scope shall not be implied from this Section.

7.3 Mutual Release. The Parties mutually agree not to bring, file, or initiate any legal action,
suit, or proceeding in any forum — administrative, judicial, or otherwise — based on any claim
of infringement of intellectual property rights that are necessarily practiced in the course of
the Research Program, provided that such conduct falls within the agreed scope of the Research
Program at the relevant time.


ARTICLE 8 — CHANGE OF CONTROL

8.1 Notification. Each Party shall provide the other Party with written notice within five (5)
business days upon becoming aware of any impending change of control, including any merger,
acquisition, consolidation, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets or equity (whether
by a single transaction or a series of related transactions).

8.2 Option to Terminate. Upon receipt of a change of control notice, the non-affected Party shall
have sixty (60) days to elect, in its sole discretion, to either: (a) continue this Agreement
under the terms set forth herein, with the successor entity assuming all obligations; or (b)
terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice without penalty.

8.3 Competing Acquirer. If a change of control results in a Party being acquired by an entity that
is a direct competitor of the other Party in the Commercialization Territory, the non-affected
Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice, regardless of the sixty (60)
day period in Section 8.2.


ARTICLE 9 — TERM AND POST-TERMINATION SERVICES

9.1 Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of
five (5) years, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article 9. The Parties may
mutually agree to extend this Agreement for additional periods.

9.2 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason upon
one hundred eighty (180) days' prior written notice.

9.3 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written
notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within
sixty (60) days of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

9.4 Post-Termination Services and Obligations. Following the expiration or termination of this
Agreement for any reason:

(a) Wind-Down Services: For a period of one hundred eighty (180) days following the termination
date, each Party shall continue to perform such activities as are reasonably necessary to conclude
ongoing experiments, preserve biological samples, and complete any research that is at a critical
juncture and cannot be paused without material loss of data or scientific value. Such post-
termination services shall be performed at each Party's own cost unless otherwise agreed in writing.

(b) Data Transfer: Each Party shall promptly provide the other Party with complete copies of all
research data, results, laboratory notebooks, and records generated under the Research Program,
in a mutually agreed electronic format.

(c) Revenue Obligations Survival: Revenue sharing obligations under Article 4 shall survive
termination and continue for as long as either Party derives Net Revenue from commercialization
of any Joint IP.

(d) Joint IP Disposition: Following termination, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith
regarding the disposition or continued exploitation of Joint IP, including whether to jointly
commercialize, license to third parties, or assign all rights to one Party.


ARTICLE 10 — FORCE MAJEURE

10.1 Force Majeure Events. Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing
its obligations under this Agreement to the extent such failure or delay results from events
beyond such Party's reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics,
epidemics, government-imposed restrictions, war, terrorism, civil unrest, or widespread failures
of third-party infrastructure or supply chains ("Force Majeure Event").

10.2 Notification Requirements. A Party experiencing a Force Majeure Event shall: (a) notify the
other Party in writing within five (5) business days of the commencement of the Force Majeure
Event; (b) describe in reasonable detail the nature, expected duration, and anticipated impact of
the event; and (c) use commercially reasonable efforts to resume performance as quickly as
practicable.

10.3 Extended Force Majeure. If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90)
consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement on thirty (30) days' written notice
without liability for the terminated obligations.


ARTICLE 11 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION AND ARBITRATION

11.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York, without
regard to its conflict of laws rules.

11.2 Good-Faith Negotiation. In the event of any dispute arising out of or relating to this
Agreement, the Parties shall first attempt to resolve the dispute through good-faith negotiation
between senior management representatives. If the dispute is not resolved within thirty (30) days
of written notice, either Party may escalate to binding arbitration.

11.3 Arbitration. Any dispute not resolved pursuant to Section 11.2 shall be submitted to binding
arbitration in accordance with the rules of the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC Arbitration
Rules). The arbitral tribunal shall consist of three (3) arbitrators, one appointed by each Party
and the third appointed by the two Party-appointed arbitrators. The seat of arbitration shall be
New York, New York. Proceedings shall be conducted in English.

11.4 Third Party Beneficiary. The Parties acknowledge that this Agreement is entered into solely
for the benefit of the Parties and their respective successors and permitted assigns. No third
party shall be a beneficiary of or have any rights under this Agreement, and no third parties
shall be entitled to rely on any provision hereof.


ARTICLE 12 — GENERAL PROVISIONS

12.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with all Exhibits, constitutes the entire
agreement of the Parties with respect to its subject matter.

12.2 Amendments. No modification of this Agreement shall be effective unless in a written
instrument executed by both Parties.

12.3 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered by hand, courier, or certified mail
to the addresses set forth in the preamble or as otherwise designated in writing.

12.4 Waiver. No failure or delay in exercising any right shall constitute a waiver thereof.

12.5 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, the remaining provisions
shall continue in full force and effect.

12.6 Counterparts and Electronic Signatures. This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, each
of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures shall have the same force and effect
as original signatures.


EXHIBIT A — RESEARCH PROGRAM DESCRIPTION

Phase 1 (Months 1–18): Biomarker Discovery
- Identification and validation of genomic biomarkers associated with early-stage solid tumors
- Helix contribution: bioinformatics platform, sequencing infrastructure, 15 FTE
- Stratum contribution: clinical sample repository (5,000+ specimens), diagnostic assay expertise, 10 FTE

Phase 2 (Months 19–36): Assay Development
- Development and optimization of multiplex sequencing assay based on Phase 1 findings
- Regulatory pre-submission meetings with FDA and EMA
- Joint contribution: equally shared resources

Phase 3 (Months 37–60): Clinical Validation
- Multi-site clinical validation study (target n=2,000)
- Regulatory submission and market authorization
- Commercial launch preparation in Commercialization Territory
